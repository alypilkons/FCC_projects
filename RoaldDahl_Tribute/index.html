Roald Dahl



"A little nonsense now and then, is cherished by the wisest men."


Roald Dahl was a screenwriter, poet, and fighter pilot, but he is probably best known for his children's books, which include Matilda, Charlie and the Chocolate Factory, James and the Giant Peach, and The Witches. His writing has been adapted into movies, television shows, and even a musical, and his books have entertained and influenced millions of children and still do today, almost thirty years after his death in 1990.
